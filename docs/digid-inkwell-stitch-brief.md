# Digid Inkwell Migration - Stitch Design Brief

Use this brief to generate design concepts for migrating `digid.ca` from WordPress/Elementor to an Inkwell-powered content site.

## Goal

Design a modern consulting and content site for Digid Inc., a Canadian digital transformation and AI advisory business. The site should feel credible to SMB owners, manufacturers, and operations leaders who need help with AI adoption, QMS, digital strategy, and government funding.

The new site is not a generic SaaS landing page. It is a business advisory site with a strong publishing layer.

## Audience

- Canadian small and mid-sized business owners
- Manufacturing and operations leaders
- Quality managers exploring QMS and ISO workflows
- Teams considering AI, automation, RAG, or digital adoption plans
- Business owners looking for grants and DMAP-style funding support

## Required Pages

Generate design direction for these page types:

- Homepage
- Services overview
- AI Solutions
- QMS Solutions
- Grants / DMAP Advisor
- Blog / resources index
- Article detail page
- Contact / consultation page

## Homepage Structure

The homepage should prioritize:

- Clear Digid positioning: digital transformation, AI, QMS, and funding advisory for Canadian SMBs
- Primary action: book a consultation
- Secondary action: explore services
- Service pathways:
  - AI Solutions
  - QMS Solutions
  - Digital Transformation
  - Grants and DMAP advisory
- Proof / credibility band:
  - Canadian business focus
  - manufacturing and SMB experience
  - funding and adoption planning
- Featured insights from the publishing layer
- Short FAQ section
- Contact call to action

## Visual Direction

Use a serious business-consulting look with technical confidence. Avoid generic startup gradients, purple-blue dominance, beige/sand palettes, and heavy card-on-card layouts.

Direction:

- precise, editorial, technical
- clean grid, strong typographic hierarchy
- restrained color with a distinct accent
- human advisory tone, not enterprise-software jargon
- content-rich sections that still scan quickly

Suggested palette:

- near-white background
- charcoal text
- deep green or steel teal accent
- signal yellow or precise red for small emphasis only
- light neutral section bands

Typography:

- use a readable editorial sans or mixed sans/serif pairing
- make article pages feel like a serious business publication
- keep line lengths readable

## Interaction And Components

Include design ideas for:

- service cards that link to content clusters
- consultation CTA band
- article/resource cards
- category/tag chips
- lead form treatment
- FAQ treatment
- case-study or proof cards
- mobile navigation

Do not hide form fields behind a modal in the primary contact flow. The contact page should support a visible consultation request form.

## Content Model Awareness

The site will be built on Inkwell. Designs should account for:

- markdown-driven articles
- tag pages across collections
- topic pages for AI, QMS, grants, manufacturing, and digital transformation
- Pagefind search
- RSS and sitemap
- structured content that can be edited from an Obsidian vault

## SEO Requirements

The design should support:

- one clear H1 per page
- visible breadcrumbs on article and service pages
- FAQ blocks that can map to FAQPage schema
- service pages that can map to Service schema
- article pages that can map to Article schema
- strong internal linking between services, topics, and articles

## Migration Constraints

The current WordPress site has:

- about 17 indexed pages
- about 48 indexed posts
- about 275 media items
- lead forms on AI, QMS, grants, and contact pages

Do not design a site that assumes all WordPress functionality disappears on day one. The design should let us migrate content first and replace lead capture cleanly.

## Output Requested From Stitch

Create:

- one homepage concept
- one service page concept
- one article page concept
- one contact page concept
- mobile layouts for homepage and contact
- a small component kit covering buttons, service cards, article cards, tags, FAQ blocks, and form fields

Use realistic copy based on Digid's current positioning, but do not copy long WordPress sections verbatim.
